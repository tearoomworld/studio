02 — the problem
source
why this exists

atlanta has a culture problem — it isn't a lack of events. it's a lack of discovery.

listening sessions, supper clubs, art shows, small concerts. they're happening. they're great. and people find out about them after they're over — in screenshots, in someone else's story, in a group chat they weren't part of.

attendees
the great stuff doesn't surface. eventbrite shows sponsored listings. instagram buries them in stories.
"i would've gone if i'd known."
hosts
they're posting on three platforms and it's still mostly friends-of-friends showing up. no growth, no analytics, no payout that doesn't take 9%.
"i love throwing it but i can't keep doing this for free."
03 — the product
source
what we built
an events app for atlanta that treats events like culture, not commerce.
editorial discovery · two-tap ticketing · host tools that don't feel like saas

source is a curated atlanta-only events app. native ios + android at launch. one place to find what's happening tonight, buy a ticket without ten layers of fees, and — if you're hosting — get the tools to make your next event bigger than your group chat.

04 — how it works
source
three motions, one app

find. buy. host.

01 — find
editorial discovery
tonight, this week, by neighborhood, by vibe. real human curation, not algorithmic mush.
launch surfaces: tonight · this weekend · supper clubs · listening · art · neighborhood pages (cabbagetown, west end, virginia-highland).
02 — buy
two-tap ticketing
apple pay in two taps. no eventbrite-tier fees stacked on top.
10% platform fee + $0.99 flat (or pass-through to attendee). transparent on every listing.
03 — host
host dashboard
a tool for the people throwing it. attendee list, payouts, recap, repeat-event templates.
stripe connect for direct payouts (no waiting). quarterly host prize pool funded by platform fees — top-rated host wins.
05 — market
source
why atlanta first

atlanta is the right wedge — culture-rich, mid-sized, underserved.

atlanta metro population
6.3M
large enough to sustain a marketplace. small enough that hosts and attendees move in overlapping circles — word of mouth still works as a growth channel.
events ticketing market · us
$32B
eventbrite, ticketmaster, partiful, posh, dice, etc. fragmented at the small-event end (under 500 attendees). that's the gap.

expansion order: atlanta → nashville → austin → new orleans. cities with strong local culture, walkable cores, and an active host class. multi-city architecture is in the v1 schema.

06 — how it makes money
source
business model

simple ticket fees. a quarter of every dollar goes back to hosts.

10%
platform fee
on every ticket. paid by the host out of price, or passed to the attendee at checkout. transparent.
$0.99
flat per-ticket
covers stripe + infrastructure. hosts can price into ticket cost. no surprise fees on either side.
25%
of fees → quarterly host prize pool
top-rated atlanta host wins it every three months. our reputation depends on hosts winning, not on us extracting.

08 — why us, why now
source
the wedge

everyone else built a marketplace. we're building a magazine that happens to sell tickets.

eventbrite · ticketmaster · partiful · dice

built for global scale. faceless infrastructure. extract value from the host.

"discover events" surfaces sponsored listings, then a search box.

no point of view. no city ever feels like itself.

fee stacks (service fee, processing, "all-in pricing" footnote) erode attendee trust.

source

local-first. atlanta only at launch. one city done well before any second.

"tonight" is a real curated page someone wrote, not an algorithm.

host wins. quarterly prize means our incentives are aligned with theirs — not extractive.

one fee, transparent, every time. no stacking.
