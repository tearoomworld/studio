As simple as turning on the TV.

1

Questions

The honest answers.

Isn't this just a screen replacement for real activities?
No. Kindred is one session a week — a visit, not a habit. Your in-person programming stays exactly as it is. Think of Kindred as the equivalent of a sister community coming over for the afternoon, except no one has to leave the building.
What hardware do we need?
A TV with HDMI input and a laptop or tablet to plug into it. Most communities already have everything required. We'll send a simple setup checklist after pilot signup.
Do residents need to know how to use technology?
Not at all. Residents never touch a device. They watch the TV, play the games on physical cards, and talk. The activities director handles everything on the screen.
Is this HIPAA-compliant?
Kindred doesn't store or process protected health information. We work with first names, room assignments, and game data — nothing clinical. We're happy to provide a Business Associate Agreement if your compliance team requires one.
What happens after the 30-day free pilot?
If you and your residents love it, you start paying month four. If not, no hard feelings — you keep all your session recaps and we wish you well. No auto-billing, no contracts.
How do you match us with other communities?
During pilot setup, we ask about your residents' eras, regions, languages, and interests. We match you with communities whose residents share enough common ground to spark real conversation — usually a similar generation in a different state.
